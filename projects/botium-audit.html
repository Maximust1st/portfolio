Security Audit

Botium Toys Security Audit & Risk Assessment

Conducted a cybersecurity audit of a simulated retail organization using the NIST Cybersecurity Framework to identify security gaps, compliance risks, and operational weaknesses.

Back to Projects

Overview

Project Summary

Performed a security audit of Botium Toys to evaluate current security controls, compliance posture, and operational risks. This assessment focused on identifying weaknesses in access management, data protection, incident preparedness, and regulatory compliance.

Risk Findings

Key Risks Identified

  • Missing least privilege access controls
  • No separation of duties
  • No encryption for customer payment data
  • No intrusion detection system (IDS)
  • No regular backups of critical business data
  • No disaster recovery plan
  • Weak password policy enforcement
  • No centralized password management system
  • Compliance gaps related to PCI DSS and GDPR

Recommendations

Security Improvements

  • Implement least privilege access controls to restrict unnecessary access to sensitive systems and customer data.
  • Enforce separation of duties to reduce insider threats and excessive permissions.
  • Encrypt customer payment and sensitive business data to improve confidentiality and compliance.
  • Strengthen password policies and implement centralized password management.
  • Deploy intrusion detection monitoring to improve threat visibility and alerting.
  • Establish a disaster recovery plan to improve business continuity during security incidents.
  • Maintain regular backups of critical systems and business data for recovery purposes.
  • Improve asset inventory and classification to better manage organizational risk.
